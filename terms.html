Project Terms

Plain English, no legalese. This is what you're agreeing to when you pay a deposit, and what I'm agreeing to when I take it. Read it — it protects both of us.

1. Scope of Work

What gets built is what's listed in your bundle, plus what we agree to in writing before the build starts. The bundle features are the scope. Anything outside them is a change request.

Before I start, we'll confirm the details over Discord or email. That written confirmation becomes the definition of "done." If it isn't in the bundle and it isn't in that confirmation, it isn't in the project.

2. Changes and Additions

Projects evolve — that's normal, and small tweaks are part of the deal. But if you ask for something that meaningfully expands the work (a new feature, a redesign, a different platform), that's a change request.

I'll quote it before doing it. You can approve it, decline it, or defer it. I won't quietly do extra work and surprise you with a bill, and I won't quietly drop things either.

3. Revisions

Each bundle includes a set number of revision rounds (listed in the bundle details). A revision round means: you review the work, send me a consolidated list of changes, I make them.

  • Revisions cover fixing and refining what we agreed to build.
  • Revisions do not cover changing what we agreed to build — that's a change request (see above).
  • Extra revision rounds beyond your bundle are billed at $45/hour.

4. Payment

  • 25% deposit up front. Work begins when it clears.
  • 75% balance on completion, due before final delivery of source code.
  • Payments run through Stripe. You'll get a receipt for each one.

The deposit is non-refundable once work has begun. It covers the time I've set aside for you and turned down other work to protect. If you cancel before I've started, I'll refund it.

5. If You Go Quiet

This one matters, so I'm being direct about it.

I can't finish a project without you — I'll need feedback, assets, and answers along the way. If I ask for something and don't hear back:

  • After 14 days of no response, the project is paused. Your deposit still holds your place.
  • After 30 days of no response, the project is considered abandoned. The deposit is forfeit and I'll move on to other work.
  • Want to restart after that? Happy to — but it's a new project and a new deposit.

I'll email you before either of those deadlines hits. You won't get blindsided.

6. If I Go Quiet

Fair is fair. If I stop responding for 14 days without warning you, you get your deposit back in full — no argument, no hassle.

If something comes up on my end (illness, emergency), I'll tell you and we'll agree on a new timeline or I'll refund you. I don't disappear.

7. Cancellation

  • You cancel before work starts: full refund of the deposit.
  • You cancel mid-build: deposit is forfeit. You receive whatever has been completed so far, as-is.
  • I cancel mid-build: full refund of the deposit, and you keep whatever's been built.

8. Ownership

When the final payment clears, you own everything. Full source code, no subscriptions, no license fees, no lock-in. Do whatever you want with it — modify it, resell it, hand it to another developer.

Until final payment, the work remains mine. I retain the right to reuse generic, non-identifying components (helper functions, boilerplate) in other projects. I will never reuse your branding, content, business logic, or data.

9. What You Provide

I build. I don't design logos or produce branding assets. You'll need to bring:

  • Logos, images, and any visual assets
  • Text and content
  • Access to any existing code, accounts, or APIs the project touches
  • Answers, when I ask questions

You confirm you have the right to use everything you send me. If you hand me copyrighted material you don't own, that's on you, not me.

10. Hosting and Third-Party Costs

The build price covers my labor. It does not cover ongoing third-party costs — hosting, domains, API fees, database services. Those are yours, in your own accounts, under your own name.

I'll set them up and show you how they work. You own them, which means nobody can hold your project hostage — including me.

11. Support After Delivery

Bundles that include post-launch support cover bug fixes — things that don't work as we agreed they would. That's free, within the window listed.

New features, new requests, and changes after delivery are billed as new work. I don't disappear after delivery, but "still around" isn't the same as "free forever."

12. Limitation of Liability

I build carefully and test my work. But software is software.

My total liability for any claim is limited to the amount you paid me for the project. I'm not liable for indirect or consequential damages — lost profits, lost data, business interruption, or downtime.

You're responsible for backing up your own data and for testing the work in your environment before relying on it in production.

13. Portfolio Rights

I'd like to show the work in my portfolio. If you'd rather I didn't, just tell me and I won't — no explanation needed, no effect on price.

I will never publish your data, credentials, or anything you've marked confidential, regardless.

14. Governing Law

These terms are governed by the laws of the State of Florida, USA.
